Interpreting Vader Graph

Vader Is 'A Parsimonious Rule-based Model for Sentiment Analysis of Social Media Text' (Hutto, C.J. & Gilbert, E.E. 2014) made available through NLTK's Python library.

Vader's scoring comprises of four values (pos, neg, neu, compound) with values ranging from -1 ➜ 1.

pos - Refers to the positivity of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the positivity score to one, the more positive the opinion.

neg - Refers to the negativity of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the negativity score to one, the harsher the opinion.

neu - Refers to the neutrality of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the neutrality score to one, the greater the inconclusiveness.

compound - Refers to the overall mood of a comment. Ranges from -1 ➜ 1. The closer the compound score to one, the more a comment was overall deemed positive. Therefore greater noise on the LHS of a graph versus the RHS indicates a skew toward overall negativity. See Firgure 1.0

Interpreting roBERTa Graph

'This is a roBERTa-base model trained on ~124M tweets from January 2018 to December 2021 and finetuned for sentiment analysis with the TweetEval benchmark.' (Hugging Face, 2021)

roBERTa's scoring comprises of three values (pos, neg, neu) with values ranging from 0 ➜ 1.

pos - Refers to the positivity of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the positivity score to one, the more positive the opinion.

neg - Refers to the negativity of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the negativity score to one, the harsher the opinion.

neu - Refers to the neutrality of a comment. Ranges from 0 - 1. The closer the neutrality score to one, the greater the inconclusiveness.
